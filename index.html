The Short Version

The four highest-leverage levers, in order of return on effort.

  1. Lock in competitive input settings — and never touch them. Controller: Dynamic aim curve, Instant ADS transition, sensitivity 6–8, ADS multiplier ~0.85–0.90, low deadzones, vibration & trigger-effects off. Mouse: 800 DPI for precision, 1600 for speed.
  2. Tune audio for footsteps. Zero out Music and War Tracks, max Effects, keep Master high, pick a footstep-forward mix (ONE Esports' "Home Theater").
  3. Run the current meta. Season 4: DS20 Mirage (#1 AR), Carbon 57 / Dravec 45 (SMGs), VS Recon (sniper) — built around fast sprint-to-fire and 50–60 round mags.
  4. Train aim deliberately. A 45-minute four-pillar daily routine (flick, track, micro-adjust, switch), progressed accuracy-first over 30 days, capped at 2–3 hrs/day.

5 · Movement Tech Unverified

The research pass did not surface fact-checked claims on 2026 BO7-era movement specifics. Below is widely-taught community practice — confirm against current in-game mechanics, which change with each engine update.
  • Plate while moving. Never stand still to armor up — re-plate behind cover or while repositioning. Do it the instant you break contact.
  • Slide & slide-cancel to shorten your hitbox and break an enemy's tracking as a fight opens.
  • Peeking: pre-aim the angle, expose minimally, and jiggle-peek to bait shots without committing.
  • Drop-shot / jump-shot selectively to throw off a crosshair mid-duel.
  • Drill movement in the firing range until it costs zero conscious thought in a real fight.
Strategy

6 · Gulag Strategy High

  • You get exactly one Gulag opportunity per match — after your first elimination, if it's still open.
  • It's a 1v1 with identical loadouts — pure aim and nerves, no gear advantage.
  • Two ways to redeploy: eliminate your opponent, or climb the ascender exfil that drops into the middle of the room during overtime (~15–20s in).
  • Hold a strong angle, don't over-peek early, and sprint the middle for the ascender if you can beat your opponent there in OT.
Source: Official Call of Duty guide (primary)

The Winning Blueprint Unverified

A complete game plan for taking the win, phase by phase. Synthesized from the strategy sources below — sound, widely-taught practice, but not individually fact-checked in this research pass.

The single mindset shift that wins games: Warzone rewards placement, not kills. The team holding the best position in the last circle wins — not the one with the most eliminations. Survive first, frag second, and let other squads burn each other down while you stay alive and well-positioned.
Phase 1

The Drop & First 90 Seconds

~0–3 min
  • Land adjacent to a hot zone, not on top of it — grab loot uncontested, then rotate into fights on your terms.
  • First priority is cash. Rush a Buy Station to purchase your custom loadout as fast as possible — your meta guns and perks decide every fight after.
  • Get to full armor and stock plates before pushing anyone.
Phase 2

Early Game — Bank Intel & Cash

first 2 circles
  • Run contracts with purpose: Recon reveals the next circle (huge rotation advantage), Bounty funds your buys, Most Wanted only if you're confident.
  • Buy UAVs to track enemy density and pick safe fights. Information beats aim at this stage.
  • Avoid greedy third fights — take engagements you can win cleanly and reset.
Phase 3

Mid Game — Own the Rotation

circles 3–5
  • Rotate early and with cover. Be inside the next circle before it closes, holding high ground and an angle — never gassed and exposed at the edge.
  • Third-party deliberately: let two squads fight, then clean up the weakened winner. Don't be the squad that gets third-partied — reposition and plate the instant a fight ends.
  • Pick up self-revives, gas masks, and a precision airstrike / cluster for endgame zone control.
  • Hold a compound with cover at your back; deny enemies the high ground you want.
Phase 4

Endgame — Closing It Out

final 2–3 circles
  • Get position before the zone forces you. Claim high ground or a strong building early — being caught moving in the open in the last circles loses games.
  • Read the circle pull and play the side it's collapsing toward, so you rotate downhill while enemies push uphill into you.
  • Use utility to win space: smokes to cross open ground, stuns/flashes before a push, gas mask to play the edge when others can't.
  • Patience wins. In a 2–3 squad endgame, let them fight first. Hold your angle, trade efficiently with your team, and take the last fight from the strongest position — not the most aggressive one.

The Roadmap

The 8-Week Average → Expert Plan

Combines the verified 30-day aim ramp with settings lock-in, meta adoption, and in-match review. About 1–2 focused hours/day.

Week1

Foundation & settings lock-in

  • Set controller/mouse, audio and FOV. Commit — no more changes.
  • Build current-meta loadouts (DS20 Mirage + Carbon 57).
  • Aim training: Week-1 ramp — slow, perfect form, 45 min/day.
Week2

Aim speed + movement basics

  • Aim ramp Week 2 — +10% speed.
  • Drill plating-on-the-move and slide-cancels in the firing range daily.
  • Play Resurgence where you can re-engage and fight often.
Week3

Aim consistency + Gulag & peeking

  • Aim ramp Week 3 — hold speed, recover accuracy.
  • Practice pre-aiming and minimal-exposure peeks.
  • Treat every Gulag as 1v1 aim practice; learn the overtime ascender.
Week4

New scenarios + positioning

  • Aim ramp Week 4 — add harder scenarios.
  • Focus on rotating early with cover and avoiding third parties.
  • Start recording your matches.
Week5

VOD review becomes routine

  • Review one death per match: positioning, plating, or peek?
  • Re-check the meta — apply any balance-patch loadout changes.
  • Aim training drops to ~30–45 min maintenance.
Week6

Comms & team roles

  • Play with a consistent squad; assign IGL / anchor / entry.
  • Drill concise callouts (direction + distance + status).
  • Practice trading kills and re-plating as a unit.
Week7

Endgame & gas management

  • Focus on circle reads, high-ground control, gas-mask plays.
  • Practice closing out final circles from advantageous spots.
  • Review every endgame loss specifically.
Week8

Integration & pressure-test

  • Combine everything in ranked/competitive play.
  • Set measurable goals (K/D, placement, accuracy %) and track them.
  • Identify your biggest remaining weakness and dedicate the next cycle to it.
